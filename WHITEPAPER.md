# Proof of Human Attention (PoHA)

**Version 0.3 · April 2026 · Author: JD (@1nimit) · License: CC BY 4.0**

---

## 1. The Problem

AI agents like OpenClaw, Microsoft Copilot, and Google Gemini now connect to email, WhatsApp, Slack, and dozens of other channels. They read, compose, and send messages autonomously. A message that took a human 5 minutes to compose is indistinguishable from one generated in 200 milliseconds.

This is not the spam problem. Spam was low-quality and filterable. AI-generated communication is contextual, personalized, and correct. The damage is dilution: when everything in an inbox looks equally considered, nothing stands out. The person who actually sat down and typed becomes invisible.

World ID proves a human owns an account — not that they composed a specific message. C2PA proves media hasn't been tampered with — not that a human invested effort creating it. AI text detectors are unreliable and trivially bypassed. Nothing tells you whether a message was typed by hand or generated by machine.

## 2. What PoHA Does

PoHA measures your typing behavior — keystroke timing, pauses, revisions, paste events — without reading what you type. If enough human effort is detected, you get a badge: a short link anyone can tap to see the proof.

```
Spent the morning debugging a race condition that
only shows up under load. Turned out to be a missing
mutex on the session cache. Three hours for one line.

✍️ Human-typed · poha.ink/k8mTx
```

The link opens a verification page showing effort band, composition duration, and timestamp. No message content is stored. No accounts required. No app needed on the receiver's end.

**On the name.** "Proof of Human Attention" describes intent, not measurement capability. What PoHA technically measures is sustained interface engagement. A bored person leaving an app open for 4 minutes, then pasting an AI draft with 3 token edits, would produce a measurable attestation despite paying little real attention. PoHA measures fingers, not minds.

**What PoHA cannot prove:** sincerity (you can type a lie with full engagement), quality (a rambling message scores higher than a concise AI response), personalization (the signals can't distinguish "composed for you" from a template), originality (you can memorize an AI draft and retype it), or truth (composition effort doesn't correlate with accuracy).

**What PoHA can prove:** that the composition process involved typing, pausing, revising, and deleting in patterns consistent with direct human input rather than automated generation or single-event pasting. That is a narrow, mechanical claim. Its usefulness depends on context.

## 3. How It Works

Five signals are measured on-device. Each is normalized to [0, 1] and weighted into a composite effort score.

| Signal | Weight | What it measures | Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Keystroke entropy | 25% | Variety in typing rhythm (Shannon entropy over inter-keystroke intervals in 50ms bins) | 0.5–3.5 bits |
| Paste ratio | 20% | Characters pasted vs typed — inverted, so less paste = higher score | 0–1.0 |
| Duration | 20% | Time from first to last input event | 3–180 seconds |
| Revision rate | 20% | Character deletions per 100 characters typed | 0–10 |
| Event density | 15% | Keystrokes per second | 0.5–3.0 |

**Bands:** `none` (< 0.1), `low` (0.1–0.3), `moderate` (0.3–0.6), `high` (0.6+). Badge requires moderate or above (0.3+).

The composite score is the only value that leaves the device by default. Individual signals are available through an opt-in full-disclosure format. This matters because individual keystroke timing patterns are effectively biometric — research on keystroke dynamics shows they can identify individuals with over 90% accuracy. Sending only the composite score collapses that fingerprinting surface.

**Attestation signing.** Each user has an Ed25519 keypair generated on-device, stored in the hardware secure enclave where available. The SDK serializes the attestation into canonical JSON and signs it. Anyone can verify the signature. The content hash binds the attestation to a specific message without storing the message itself. Timestamps are rounded to the nearest hour to limit work-pattern inference. Monthly key rotation is recommended.

**Device equity.** Sub-$100 Android phones common in India lack hardware secure enclaves. PoHA does not expose attestation tier to recipients. A supplier typing on a ₹7,000 phone gets the same visible badge as someone on a ₹80,000 phone, if the behavioral signals cross the threshold. The assurance level differs internally; the display doesn't.

## 4. Boundaries

**PoHA's usefulness is inversely proportional to message volume.** At 20 messages per day, faking PoHA costs more than just typing. At 2,000 messages per day, faking is cheaper than authentic composition. This is a design boundary, not a flaw.

PoHA works differently depending on context. In B2B commerce, hiring, and professional communication, it works as a trust signal — the recipient checks the badge because the stakes warrant it. On social media, it works as an identity signal — the sender displays the badge because it says something about how they communicate. The verification link is there for the curious, but the value is the visible mark. In high-volume outbound (sales sequences, mass email), PoHA breaks regardless of framing.

**Any metric tied to incentives becomes a target.** This is PoHA's central adversarial challenge.

| Attack | Cost | Effectiveness | Defense |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paste AI output | Zero | Caught immediately | Strong (paste_ratio = 1.0) |
| Type out AI draft manually | 3–5 min/msg | Indistinguishable from genuine | None — philosophical boundary |
| Keystroke replay scripts | Low | Moderate | Device attestation raises cost |
| Macro tools with jitter | Low | Moderate | Calibration data improves detection |
| Click farms (real humans) | ₹5–15/msg | High | Only viable at scale, where PoHA already concedes |
| Rooted phone + SDK bypass | Moderate | High | App integrity attestation |

PoHA's defenses raise the cost of faking. They don't eliminate it. The protocol does not claim to work where faking is cheaper than typing. Attack papers are welcome — they improve the scoring.

**Privacy.** A stream of attestations from the same key reveals effort patterns, active hours, and communication cadence. Composite-by-default limits the behavioral surface. Key rotation limits linkability. Hourly timestamp rounding reduces work-pattern inference. Platforms that process attestations at scale will accumulate metadata regardless — regulatory frameworks (GDPR, India's DPDP Act) are the appropriate controls for that, not protocol design.

## 5. The Protocol

The signal engine, scoring algorithm, attestation format, and verification service are open source under MIT license. The protocol specification and scoring formula are published under CC BY 4.0. Anyone can build a compatible client.
